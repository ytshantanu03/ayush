Sewage Treatment Plants

The MBBR system is a great improvement over conventional extend Aeration or Activated Sludge plants , provides a robust and compact unit in a fully design. sewage is a mixture of domestic and industrial wastes. it is more than 99% water, but the remainder contains some ions, suspended solids and harmful bacteria that must be removed before the water is released into the sea. sewage treatment refers to the process of removing contaminants , micro- organisms and other type of pollutants from wastewater. wastewater , or raw sewage , is water that drains from toilets , sinks, showers, baths, washing machine and liquid industrial waste. it is treated in a multi stage process that make it safe to return to the environment.

The wastewater undergoes several stages in its municipal treatment process for environmental protection. It includes physical, biological and sometimes chemical processes to remove pollutants. Its aim is to produce an environmentally safe sewage water, called effluent, and a solid waste, called sludge or biosolids, suitable for disposal or reuse. Reuse is often for agricultural purposes, but more recently, sludge is being used as a fuel source.

Conventional STP (MBBR/SBR)

In the conventional STP we are providing both MBBR & SBR processes. First, with conventional sewage treatment plants, the solids are separated from the wastewater stream. Then dissolved biological matter is progressively converted into a solid mass by using indigenous, water-borne micro-organisms.

Finally, the biological solids are neutralized then disposed of or re-used, and the treated water may be disinfected chemically or physically (for example by lagoons and microfiltration). In this conventional STP, most parts of the STP will be constructed in civil which requires more manpower. Water gets treated in three stages: primary, secondary & tertiary. The output water norms from the package STP and conventional STP are the same.

The final effluent can be discharged into a stream, river, bay, lagoon or wetland, or it can be used for the irrigation of a golf course, greenway or park. If it is sufficiently clean, it can also be used for groundwater recharge or agricultural purposes.

Insitu Nallah Treatment

Ecological Treatment System

On-site Engineered Wetland based Natural Treatment System (NTS) for in-situ nallah water (drain water) treatment. The NTS is a modular system that can be constructed beside the drain occupying minimum space and providing low cost efficient treatment.

The NTS is engineered ecosystem with plants and microbial culture as active biological component and stone aggregate filter media that acts as a passive physical support for the ecosystem. The active biological and passive physical components degrade pollutants and uptake nutrients in wastewater.

Each system can be tailored to specific case designed to withstand extreme fluctuation, and to last. The main objective of NTS is to provide a simple, feasible, practically sound, eco-friendly, maintenance free and cost-effective solution, which can handle the magnitude of sewage flowing through drains and treatment leading to pollution control.

Key Features

  • Treatment efficiencies for the removal of BOD, COD and fecal coliforms are in the secondary treatment range
  • NTS mostly works on gravitational flow
  • It is modular and can be tailored to suit the site conditions
  • The life cycle cost is low and can be maintained with minimum attention
  • The treated water from each unit will be discharged in the existing drain for further conveyance
  • Settling basins along with screens for floating material to be provides before the NTS system both for mine water and sewage

Composter

Rapid Aerobic Composter is scalable from household to community to small town. Aeration systems provide no foul smell operation. No vector nuisance while allowing free aeration. Accepts almost all biodegradable waste dry or wet.

Specialized microbial culture makes operation rapid thereby reducing footprint. A very dry, light, and odourless compost excellent as soil conditioner is obtained. No specific skills are required for operation and maintenance.

Functioning of Composter

  • Household biodegradable waste is to be fed to the composter in batches at different times
  • Feeding is to be continued for 8-10 days with periodic addition of specialized microbial culture
  • After 10 days, transfer mass to maturation section (takes 5-10 minutes)
  • Once empty, feeding can restart immediately
  • Compost matures in 15-18 days and is ready for use
